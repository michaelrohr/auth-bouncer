DecisionEngine — Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-04-27 · Last updated: 2026-04-27

Introduction

DecisionEngine is a privacy-first iOS app that reads your fitness and recovery data and computes a daily training recommendation. All processing happens on your iPhone. There is no backend server, no analytics SDK, no cloud sync, and no third-party data broker. This document explains exactly what data the app touches, why, and your rights as a user under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / DSG).

What data the app reads

Every category below is opt-in. The app cannot access any of it without your explicit permission, granted via iOS' system permission sheets.

  • Apple Health data — sleep, heart-rate variability (HRV), resting heart rate, workouts, mindful minutes, body weight (when available), and similar training-relevant metrics. Read via Apple's HealthKit framework. The data lives in Apple's HealthKit store on your device; we do not copy it elsewhere.
  • Workouts from optional third-party providers — when you connect Strava, Polar, WHOOP, Garmin, Wahoo, or Suunto via the in-app Settings → Data sources flow, the app pulls your activity list from that provider's API on your behalf. The OAuth scopes we request are read-only (we cannot post on your behalf, edit, or delete any activity on the provider's side).
  • Manual check-in input — optional subjective signals you type in (RPE, sleep quality, soreness). Stored locally; never leaves your device.
  • Diagnostics — Apple's MetricKit delivers crash reports and performance metrics on-device, ~24 hours after the event. We log a subset to a local NDJSON file in your app's Application Support directory. No personally identifiable information; only event names, timestamps, and feature-flag values.

How we use it

The app feeds the data above into an on-device decision engine that:

  • Computes daily readiness (HRV trend, sleep debt, training load).
  • Suggests the type and intensity of today's training session.
  • Tracks your goals and prescribes workouts that align with them.

The decision is rendered in the app and, optionally, exported to your Apple Watch via WorkoutKit. None of this data leaves your device.

Storage and security

  • SwiftData store (workouts, recommendations, history) — local file, encrypted at rest by iOS.
  • OAuth tokens (Strava, Polar, WHOOP, Garmin, Wahoo, Suunto) — stored in iOS Keychain with the kSecAttrAccessibleAfterFirstUnlockThisDeviceOnly attribute, scoped to your user identity. The token never appears in logs or exports.
  • Diagnostics file — Application Support directory, plain NDJSON. You can wipe it from Settings → Privacy at any time.

The app has no network endpoints of its own. Outbound HTTPS calls go directly to Apple's services, the provider you've authenticated with (e.g. strava.com), or the OAuth bouncer page (michaelrohr.github.io/auth-bouncer) which forwards the OAuth callback to the app's custom URL scheme. The bouncer page does not log or store anything.

Third parties

The app interacts directly with the following services on your behalf, using credentials you grant via iOS' OAuth flow. Their privacy policies apply to your account on their platform:

  • Apple HealthKit — first-party, on-device.
  • Strava — when you connect.
  • Polar — when you connect.
  • WHOOP — when you connect.
  • Garmin — when you connect.
  • Wahoo — when you connect.
  • Suunto — when you connect.

We do not share, sell, redistribute, or commercially exploit data fetched from these providers. The data is used solely to inform your daily training recommendation and stays on your device.

Data retention

Data persists on your device until you delete it. Three deletion paths:

  • Disconnect a provider — Settings → Data sources → tap the provider → Disconnect. Drops the OAuth token from Keychain immediately. The app stops pulling new activities on the next refresh; previously-fetched activities stay in your local SwiftData store unless you also wipe diagnostics (next bullet).
  • Wipe diagnostics — Settings → Privacy → Reset diagnostics.
  • Delete the app — uninstalling DecisionEngine from iOS removes the SwiftData store and Keychain entries automatically.

You can also revoke API access at the provider's side: e.g. strava.com/settings/apps for Strava. Doing so invalidates the access token; the app surfaces the revocation on the next sync.

Your rights (GDPR / Swiss FADP)

Under both regulations you have the right to:

  • Access — every byte of data the app holds is on your device; you control it directly.
  • Rectification — manual check-in fields are editable; provider data flows from the provider — correct it there.
  • Erasure — see "Data retention" above.
  • Portability — Settings → Privacy → Export diagnostics generates a JSON bundle.
  • Withdraw consent — any HealthKit category and any third-party provider can be revoked at any time without affecting other connections.

Legal basis: consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR; Art. 31 Swiss FADP) for HealthKit and provider OAuth, plus legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) for on-device diagnostics required to deliver the app's core function.

Children's privacy

DecisionEngine is not directed at children under 13. Users between 13 and 16 should obtain parental consent before installing, in line with GDPR's age-of-digital-consent rules in their member state.
